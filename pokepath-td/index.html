    PokePath TD Guide — Play, Builds & Strategy

    Quickstart — Getting Started

    PokePath TD is a community-made Pokémon tower defense fangame that emphasizes team composition and route control. On your first playthrough, familiarize yourself with the interface: resource counters, wave preview and unit slots. Early on, prioritize unlocking units with area control or slow effects — they significantly improve early wave survival.

    Recommended Team Builds

    Starter Builds

    For beginners, a reliable starter combo is an area-of-effect unit paired with a single-target high damage unit (for example, Water area + Fire single target). This balance helps clear mobs while tackling elite enemies.

    Build Specializations

    As you progress, choose a specialization: Control (slow/CC focused), Siege (high single-target DPS), or Sustain (healing/regen). Each specialization shifts upgrade priority and placement strategy.

    Maps & Wave Counters

    Node Priority

    Place core units at choke points that cover the most path length and ensure your ranged units can target multiple lanes. For air enemies, add anti-air units or flying counters to maintain coverage.

    Counter Strategies

    Use high-penetration or damage-over-time units against armored or shielded enemies. For large swarms, rely on area-clear units and staggered placement to avoid overlap waste.

    Economy & Upgrade Rhythm

    Manage your resources carefully. Avoid spreading upgrades across many low-level units; instead, concentrate upgrades on a few key units that will scale well into later waves. Time your upgrades to coincide with tougher upcoming waves shown in the preview.

    Advanced Play & Community Resources

    Follow community guides and shared builds to learn high-tier strategies and map-specific counters. Community forums and videos often reveal optimized routes and build orders for top scores.
